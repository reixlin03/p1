Project Overview

This project analyzes how Mass Transit Railway (MTR) stations influence demographic changes in Hong Kong's Tertiary Planning Units (TPUs) across census years (2001-2016). The study examines population, age, income, education, and housing characteristics in relation to MTR proximity and station openings.

Research Questions

  • Proximity Effects: Do TPUs closer to MTR stations show different demographic characteristics?
  • Temporal Effects: How do demographics change after MTR station openings?
  • Cumulative Effects: Do TPUs with longer MTR access show greater changes?
  • Spatial Patterns: Are there spatial clusters of demographic change?
  • Line-Specific Effects: Do different MTR lines have different impacts?
  • Control Analysis: Are changes due to MTR or other factors?

Data Sources

  • TPU Boundaries: Esri China Open Data Portal / Hong Kong Planning Department (2001, 2006, 2011, 2016)
  • MTR Stations: Scraped from Wikipedia and verified with OpenStreetMap
  • Demographic Data: Hong Kong Census and Statistics Department
